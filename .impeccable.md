## Design Context

### Users
HVAC contractors and their sales teams. Non-technical small business owners who sit across kitchen tables from homeowners presenting quotes. They need to trust the software immediately — if it looks flimsy or confusing, they won't use it. Context: mobile and tablet in the field, desktop at the office.

### Brand Personality
**Confident. Crafted. Grounded.**

Pipeworks is built by someone who's been in the trade. The brand should feel like a competent peer — not a Silicon Valley startup, not a legacy enterprise tool. The wizard mascot adds character and warmth without being whimsical. "Software so good it feels like magic" is the undercurrent, not the headline style.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Current phase:** Clean, simple, restrained. Minimal palette (black, white, grays). Let the content and typography do the work.
- **Future direction (locked, not yet applied):** Warm cream backgrounds, copper accents, violet product color, tinted neutrals. This palette is approved but not active on the marketing site yet.
- **Typography:** Druk Wide Bold for display headings (distinctive, bold, uppercase). Inter for body text (clean, readable).
- **Theme:** Light mode only. No dark mode.
- **References the founder likes:** Clay (clay.com), Summit (usesummit.com), Linear, Vercel — for their confidence and clarity, not their specific aesthetics.
- **Anti-references:** Generic SaaS templates, parchment/fantasy aesthetics, overly playful startup vibes.

### Design Principles
1. **Simplicity first.** The current site is intentionally minimal. Don't add visual complexity — earn every element.
2. **Trade credibility.** A 45-year-old HVAC contractor should trust this company on sight. Clean, professional, no gimmicks.
3. **Content does the work.** Brand personality comes through in language, structure, and layout — not decoration.
4. **Accessibility is non-negotiable.** WCAG AA minimum. Real form feedback, proper semantics, usable on any device.
5. **Evolve, don't revolutionize.** The warm brand palette is coming. Current changes should be compatible with that future direction without depending on it.
